Benedikt Boecking

I am a PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University, where I am a member of the Auton Lab advised by Artur Dubrawski. I am interested in the technical and theoretical aspects of how we engage domain experts in building and training machine learning models. My current research focus is to develop methods for learning from domain knowledge via rich forms of feedback (e.g. weak supevision) and guided data exploration (semi-supervised clustering). In the past, I have also worked on algorithms, tools, and data analysis to help fight sex trafficking using deep web and dark web data.
